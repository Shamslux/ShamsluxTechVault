Matthew does not know it

This is Matthew. He owns a small supermarket. Matthew is very dedicated to his business and he has some questions after participating in several entrepreneurship improvement courses. He discovered that the current world is driven by data, and that is how the largest companies in the world ensure their place in the competitive business landscape.

Now that he has been exposed to new ideas, Matthew has heard about a topic called Business Intelligence. He learned that this methodology, according to what he saw in the courses, allows for answers to dilemmas that he only now realizes he did not fully understand in his own business.

How much? How many? What kind of?

Matthew discovered that he lacks precise information and details about his business. He doesn’t know much about which products sell the most, which brands perform better, and which would be worth keeping in stock. He is also unaware of the trends among his consumers. He realized that his revenue, cash flow, and other financial details could be more automated and accurate, far beyond what he has been trying to achieve using personal spreadsheets to monitor his business.

A Broad View of the Business

Finally, Matthew hired the services of a BI Analyst. After a few meetings, Matthew explained how his business worked and what he wanted to learn more about. He recalled the information and tips from the courses he had taken and the example dashboards he had seen in presentations. The BI Analyst took several notes and brought some constructive questions about the business. After this phase, he carried out his work, using the best tools and methodologies tailored to Matthew’s business needs.

After the agreed-upon working time, Matthew was very pleased with the final result. Now, he finally had answers to his questions, and visually, he could see several charts displaying information that was previously presented in a less user-friendly way through tables and more tables in various spreadsheets.

Matthew was able to experience progress in his business by investing in building a robust data project tailored to his business’s type and size (after all, it wouldn’t make sense to aim for a multinational infrastructure for a small business, right?).

This simple step is essential in a business's journey toward data maturity. After all, no business is born data-mature—it’s a process. However, over time, by analyzing the business's history, Matthew will be able to make more sound and informed long-term decisions. And with further maturity, who knows? We might even see him using the services of a data scientist. Why not?

Congratulations, Matthew! Your business will certainly soar even higher!
