SPIRIT

BI INTERN

I started my career in the Data field after transitioning from Education at SPIRIT, a retailer based in Rio de Janeiro that specializes in premium fans. My internship focused on creating the BI project for the company and being under the guidance of the company's Business Analyst. It was a fruitful period for learning about business rules and generating various reports, ranging from those involving products, product returns, product categories, to financial reports, employee reports, and more. I worked with Power BI, MySQL database, and an environment in AWS. I also used Excel extensively due to the lack of a robust and mature Data department, but this experience was invaluable for business analysis, a type of experience not easily obtained through online courses, for example. I am grateful for the opportunity at SPIRIT that launched the beginning of my career.

OWSE

BUSINESS INTELLIGENCE ANALYST

After my internship at SPIRIT, I had the opportunity to work at OWSE, a small IT consulting firm. I worked for the client Login Logística, a large logistics and freight transportation company in Brazil. Together with my team, I was responsible for ETL, initially using Pentaho Data Integration, and later we started using Apache Hop. I maintained Power BI dashboards and worked with relational databases such as SQL Server, Oracle, Sybase, etc. I participated in the restructuring of the client's project, refactoring dimensions and facts, and ensuring that the new project complied with BI methodology rules. I spent 1 year and 4 months at this company, and I am deeply grateful to my colleagues and upper management for the opportunity. It was a period during which I received 4 promotions.

Accenture

DATA ENGINEER

I left OWSE for a learning opportunity at Accenture, as I transitioned from the BI Analyst subfield to Data Engineer. There, I participated in a squad that provided migration services, one of the largest migrations in the world from CDP to CDI (Cloudera), involving over 10,000 ETL processes. I worked with the ETL tool Jiang ETL (JETL), Python, some adjustments in Scala, Informatica PowerCenter, SQL, Hive, Impala, HDFS, and more. It was a temporary contract, and I stayed for only a few months, but it was a period during which I acquired skills that would later be useful in my subsequent work. Here, I send my regards to all my teammates, wonderful people with whom I still maintain regular contact today—great engineers and amazing individuals!.

RAÍZEN

ANAYTICS DATA ENGINEER

My current company, Raízen, is a milestone in my career. Firstly, because it is a giant in the Brazilian energy sector, which brings me great pride as a worker and as a Brazilian. Additionally, Raízen is at the forefront of technological innovations and has a robust Data maturity. I get to work with incredible people and use challenging tools, methodologies, and technologies. We utilize Airflow, PySpark, dbt, and have an infrastructure that leverages AWS and Azure, with a robust Data Lake, best practices, and IT governance always present, along with top-notch cybersecurity practices. I feel increasingly excited at the company to study new concepts in the field, and we have monthly Tech Talks featuring updates from the IT universe. I am very grateful to God for my current opportunity at this company and for the brilliant and kind colleagues with whom I can contribute and learn a great deal.
